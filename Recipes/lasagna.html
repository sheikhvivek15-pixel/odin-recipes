A Classic Italian Comfort Dish

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of wide pasta sheets, rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta, and melted cheese. Originating from the region of Emilia‑Romagna in Italy, it is baked in the oven until the layers blend together into a hearty, flavorful meal. One of the most famous versions is Lasagna alla Bolognese, which includes ragù meat sauce, béchamel, and Parmesan cheese. Lasagna is popular worldwide because it is comforting, filling, and can be prepared with many variations, including vegetarian options.
